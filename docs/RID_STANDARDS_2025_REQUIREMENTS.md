# RID Standards & 2025 Professional Studies Category Requirements

## Overview

This document summarizes the Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf (RID) Certification Maintenance Program (CMP) requirements effective December 1, 2025, including the revised Professional Studies (PS) categories.

---

## Certification Maintenance Cycle

### Cycle Duration
- **Standard cycle**: 4 years (January 1 - December 31)
- **First cycle for newly certified**: Up to 5+ years
- All subsequent cycles: 4 calendar years

### CEU Requirements Per Cycle

| Requirement | CEUs | Notes |
|-------------|------|-------|
| **Total Required** | 8.0 | Minimum for certification maintenance |
| **Professional Studies (PS)** | 6.0 minimum | Must be interpreting-related |
| **Power, Privilege & Oppression (PPO)** | 1.0 minimum | Counts toward PS total |
| **General Studies (GS)** | 2.0 maximum | Only 2 of 8 can be GS |

### Specialist Certificate Requirements
| Certificate | Additional Requirement |
|-------------|----------------------|
| SC:L (Legal) | 2.0 CEUs in legal studies |
| SC:PA (Performing Arts) | 2.0 CEUs in performing arts |

## Professional Studies Categories (2025 Revision)

### 1. Language and Cultural Development
**Focus**: Language, culture, and human behavior related to interpreting work.

**Topics Include**:
- Studies of Language Mechanics and Linguistics
  - Phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, pragmatics, discourse
  - Language variation and language change
- Sociolinguistics
  - Language and power
  - Language and cognition
  - Language in society
- Languaculture and Cultural Studies
  - Deaf culture and subcultures
  - American culture and subcultures
  - Religious studies
  - Intracultural/Intercultural dynamics
- Advanced Language Development
  - ASL and English (advanced only)
  - Other languages (Protactile, Spanish, etc.)

**Note**: Certified ASL/English interpreters may NOT earn PS CEUs for undergraduate-level ASL classes.

---

### 2. Settings-Based Studies
**Focus**: Environments where interpreters work and specialized settings.

**Topics Include**:
- Educational settings (K-12, higher education, Deaf education philosophies)
- Rehabilitation Services settings
- Legal settings (courtroom protocol, mediation, proceedings vs. table interpreters)
- Medical/Mental health settings (terminology, anatomy, patient communication)
- Substance abuse recovery programs
- Technical areas
- Virtual/remote interpreting settings
- Ethics applied in specialized settings

---

### 3. Cognitive Processes of Interpreting
**Focus**: The mental process of interpretation.

**Topics Include**:
- Message analysis and meaning extraction
- Nonlinguistic representation of meaning
- Interpreting strategies (consecutive, simultaneous, escorting)
- Working memory and its applications
- Problem-solving in interpretation
- Decision-making processes
- Cognitive flexibility

**Note**: This is distinct from language study - focuses on the interpreting process itself.

---

### 4. Professional Interpersonal Interactions
**Focus**: How interpreters interact with colleagues and consumers.

**Topics Include**:
- Teaming and collaboration
- Social etiquette navigation
- Mentoring frameworks and techniques
- Power and empowerment dynamics
- Ethical behavior in complex social situations
- Supporting emerging interpreters

**Mentoring Note**:
- Mentors must identify their OWN learning goals (not just mentee's)
- Mentees can earn CEUs for exploring mentoring strategies
- Both parties must reflect on their own growth

---

### 5. Ethical Considerations
**Focus**: Moral judgment and ethical frameworks.

**Topics Include**:
- Ethical decision-making in specific settings
- Exploration of ethical frameworks
- Personal ethical framework development and application
- RID Code of Professional Conduct

---

### 6. Supporting Knowledge and Skills
**Focus**: Professional activities not directly related to interpreting act.

**Topics Include**:
- **Theoretical Studies**
  - Theories of learning, development, change
  - Interpreting models and theories
  - Translation theories
  - Mentoring frameworks
  - History of interpretation
- **Translation Work**
  - Best practices
  - Differences between interpreting and translation
  - Emerging trends
- **Business of Interpreting**
  - Business practices
  - Tax law for independent contractors
- **Technology & Trends**
  - Remote interpreting technology
  - AI impact on interpreting
- **Professional Organizations**
  - Leadership frameworks
  - Credentialing processes

---

### 7. Studies of Healthy Minds and Bodies
**Focus**: Interpreter wellness and sustainability.

**Topics Include**:
- **Physical Studies**
  - Ergonomics
  - Physical problems interpreters face
  - Remedies and prevention
  - Physical manifestation of trauma
  - Physical wellness planning
- **Mental and Cognitive Studies**
  - Stress and trauma effects on thinking
  - Mental clarity maintenance
  - Brain health
  - Cognitive wellness planning
- **Social and Emotional Well-being**
  - Emotional impact on decision-making
  - Emotional distress indicators
  - Vicarious trauma
  - Boundary-making
  - Emotional wellness planning

**NOT Eligible** (personal activities):
- Yoga classes
- Massages
- Gym visits
- Personal meditation
- Personal therapy sessions

---

### 8. Power, Privilege, and Oppression (PPO)
**Focus**: Systems of power, privilege, bias, and oppression.

**Minimum Requirement**: 1.0 CEU per cycle

**Topics Include**:
- Systems of oppression (ableism, ageism, audism, classism, racism, sexism, etc.)
- Dynamics of oppression (institutionalized, prejudice, bullying)
- Stereotyping and marginalization
- Social justice and liberation studies
- Cultural competence
- Identity and intersectionality
- Trauma-informed interpreting
- Accountability

**PPO Programming Must**:
1. Practice identifying systems of power, privilege, oppression
2. Engage in conversations about dismantling oppressive systems
3. Develop practice for assessing contexts and wielding privilege responsibly

**Target Groups** (not exhaustive):
- BIPOC individuals
- Deaf, DeafBlind, DeafDisabled, Hard-of-Hearing, Coda, Late-Deafened communities
- People with disabilities
- Neurodivergent individuals
- Women
- Religious minorities
- LGBTQIA+ community
- Immigrants and New Americans

**Note**: Simply sharing lived experiences is NOT sufficient for PPO designation.

## General Studies (GS)

**Definition**: Topics that enhance general/world knowledge but aren't directly interpreting-related.

**Limit**: Maximum 2.0 CEUs count toward the 8.0 requirement.

**Requirements**: Must still have clearly defined educational goals and measurable objectives.

---

## CEU Calculation

### Standard Activities
- **1 CEU = 10 contact hours** of direct instruction
- **0.1 CEU = 1 contact hour**

### Non-Traditional Activities
- Reading, videos, immersion: typically **2:1 or 3:1 ratio** (hours to credit)
- Sponsor must document rationale for CEU calculation

### Academic Coursework
- **1 quarter credit = 1.0 CEU** (10 contact hours)
- **1 semester credit = 1.5 CEUs** (15 contact hours)
- Minimum grade: C (2.0) or Pass

### Teaching CEUs
- General Studies CEUs for presenting
- Awarded only once per activity per cycle

---

## Activity Types

### 1. Sponsor Initiated Activities (SIA)
Workshops, conferences, webinars developed by RID Approved Sponsors.

### 2. Participant Initiated Non-RID Activities (PINRA)
Professional development from non-RID organizations (must be pre-approved).

### 3. Academic Coursework (AC)
Credit courses from accredited institutions.

### 4. Independent Study (IS)
Self-designed learning experiences (max 2.0 CEUs per study).

---

## Unacceptable Activities

CEUs will NOT be awarded for:
- Board/Committee activities
- Association meetings
- Professional interpreting work
- Social events (lunch, dinner, networking)
- Employer-required trainings
- Company-proprietary processes
- Introductory ASL/English/interpreting courses

---

## Documentation Requirements

### For All Activities
- Activity title and description
- Educational objectives (measurable, observable)
- Presenter qualifications
- Content area designation
- CEU calculation rationale
- Attendance verification
- Evaluation summary

### Record Retention
- **6 years minimum** (effective January 1, 2025)
- Accounts for 5+ year first cycles

---

## Key Dates & Deadlines

### Sponsor Dues
- **June 30**: Dues payment deadline
- **July 1**: Suspension if unpaid (+ late fees)
- **August 1**: Revocation if still unpaid

### Program Review (Annual)
- **March 15**: Notice sent to sponsors
- **June 15**: Materials submission deadline
- **September 15**: Results released
- **October 31**: Corrective Action Plans due (if required)

## Implications for Our Platform

### Workshop Tagging System
Each workshop must be tagged with:
1. **Content Area**: PS or GS
2. **PS Category** (if applicable): 1-8 from above
3. **PPO Designation**: Yes/No
4. **Specialist Relevance**: Legal, Performing Arts, or None
5. **CEU Value**: Calculated based on contact hours
6. **Content Knowledge Level**: Little/None, Some, Extensive, Teaching

### Interpreter Progress Tracking
Track per interpreter:
- Total CEUs earned in current cycle
- PS CEUs earned
- PPO CEUs earned
- GS CEUs earned
- Specialist CEUs (if applicable)
- Cycle start/end dates
- Extension status (one-time lifetime)

### Compliance Alerts
- Warning when approaching cycle end with insufficient CEUs
- Category-specific deficiency alerts
- Specialist requirement tracking
